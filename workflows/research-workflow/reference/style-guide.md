**Context:** Internal meeting-prep handbook for Axcion co-leads building conversational fluency with Nordic mid-market PE fund partners.

**Audience:** Patrik and Daniel — intellectually sharp, analytically sophisticated, but with no practical PE experience. All PE terminology should be defined on first use through natural, contextual explanation woven into the prose. The text should be fully self-contained: a reader with no PE background should never need to stop and look something up.

**Key output:** Reader can discuss PE's structural features, ecosystem tensions, and performance evidence with the nuance expected of an informed counterpart — including the persistence finding as the strongest meeting-ready insight.

## Voice Notes

- Academic textbook register: authoritative but accessible, teaches as it goes, never assumes the reader will just "get it"
- Define all PE terms on first use through contextual explanation, not parenthetical glosses
- Natural prose flow with varied sentence length and paragraph breathing
- No em dashes
- When a finding matters, give it a beat before moving on
- Every sentence must either (a) state a fact, (b) explain a concept, or (c) make a concrete analytical point. If a sentence does none of these, delete it.
- No meta-commentary about meeting utility or fluency signalling. The text teaches; the reader decides how to use it.
- No dramatic framing or rhetorical teasing. State conclusions when you reach them. Do not preview them, tease them, or announce that something important is coming.
- No "sounds wise" generalisations. If a sentence could apply to any industry or any report, it is too vague. Make it specific to PE or cut it.
- Transitions should be functional, not rhetorical. "The next question is whether these returns justify the trade-offs" is functional. "This reshapes the conversation into something more pointed" is rhetorical. Use the first kind. Delete the second kind.
- No meeting coaching. Do not tell the reader how to use information in conversations, what signals fluency, or what position to take.
- No rhetorical questions or dramatic pivots. State findings and conclusions directly.
- No announcement sentences. Do not tell the reader what the section is about to do. Just do it.
